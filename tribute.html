Pierre Nkurunziza

The man who ended Burundi's longest civil war

Here's a time line of Pierre Nkurunziza's life:

  • 1963 - Born in Bujumbura, Burundi
  • 1990 - Graduated from the University of Burundi in Bujumbura with a degree in Physical education. He went on to teach high school and also served as an assistant at the University.
  • 1995 - Nkurunziza narrowly avoided death during a 1995 army attack on the university campus that killed some 200 people. After his escape Nkurunziza became active in the conflict, joining the Forces for the Defense of Democracy (Forces pour la Défense de Démocratie; FDD), which was the armed wing of a Hutu exile group, the National Council for the Defense of Democracy (Conseil National pour la Défense de Démocratie; CNDD)
  • 1998 - Sentenced to death in absentia for his rebel activities by the Burundian court
  • 2001 - Nkurunziza assumes chairmanship of CNDD-FDD.
  • 2003 - In that capacity Nkurunziza headed negotiations that culminated in his faction of the CNDD-FDD signing a peace accord with Pres. Domitien Ndayizeye. As part of the agreement, Nkurunziza received immunity from prosecution for war crimes.
  • 2004 - Joins Ndayizeye’s cabinet as minister of good governance.
  • 2005 - Nkurunziza’s CNDD-FDD becomes an official political party.
  • 2005 - Elected president of Burundi, In the ensuing vote by members of Parliament, Nkurunziza, the only candidate, won 151 of the 162 ballots cast and was elected president on August 19, 2005. He was formally sworn into office on August 25.
  • 2006 - Nkurunziza successfully ushered Burundi into the East African Community economic bloc and in April 2007 aided in the reformation of the Economic Community of the Great Lakes Countries, a trade organization including Burundi, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, and Rwanda. With the aid of World Bank funds, he also spearheaded infrastructure projects aimed at making water and electricity more accessible.
  • 2015 - Nkurunziza won a third term on July 21, 2015.
  • 2018 - Nkurunziza was granted the title of “eternal supreme guide” by the CNDD-FDD.
  • 2020 - He declined to stand for another term in office. His denial appeared to be bolstered when, in January 2020, the CNDD-FDD designated Evariste Ndayishimiye their candidate for the presidential election scheduled for later that year; Ndayishimiye was declared the winner of the May election. Nkurunziza was scheduled to step down from the presidency in August, but he died unexpectedly in June.
  • 2020 - Dies at the age of 55.
